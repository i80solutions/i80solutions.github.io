About

I love cooking - not by the book, but spontaneously, guided by intuition, memory, and whatever ingredients are around. Over the years, I've built up a rich personal history: dishes invented on the fly, flavor combinations that surprised me, moments of improvisation captured in photos, notes, and conversations.

Lately, I've been asking a new kind of question: What if I could talk to a system that remembers all of that? What if I could ask: “What did I make that winter night with salmon and miso?” or “What did I cook when the fridge was almost empty?”

I'm now building a personal knowledge base - not of recipes, but of cooking stories - so that AI can recall not just ingredients, but intent, memory, and style. This is about more than food. It's the first step toward a larger dream: creating a system that captures how I think, what I know, and how I speak - something that feels like an extension of my own mind.

The long-term vision is deeply personal. Imagine an AI that doesn't just answer questions - it reflects you. One that understands your thoughts, ideas, preferences, memories - because it's built from your own lived experience. A system that recalls what you've said, what you've cooked, what you've believed, and how you've expressed yourself.

I'm working toward a kind of virtual “me” - not just for convenience, but for continuity. So that even years from now, I could still talk to myself from the past. So that others could ask, “What would Alex have said?”, and get a meaningful response.

The same technology behind this - a structured knowledge base powered by Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG) - isn't limited to personal use. It can power organizations that need trustworthy, domain-specific memory: guest service platforms, internal support teams, healthcare systems, and more. In these environments, public LLMs tend to hallucinate - because they don't know what only you know.

This is the mission behind i80solutions, a personal research initiative focused on building AI that is precise, context-aware, and grounded in truth. This research will explore how we can bridge the expressive power of large language models with the reliability of curated, private-domain knowledge. Through hands-on experiments, log-driven learning, and systematic design, I'm building a system that prioritizes truth over fluency, precision over speculation, and usefulness over performance.

Whether it's remembering a dish, answering a guest's question, or preserving the way someone thinks long after they're gone - this is about building AI that remembers like we do. It's a difficult challenge. But that's the road I'm on.

- Alex Wang
October 29, 2024

How It Works

i80solutions combines a carefully curated knowledge base, vector search optimized for real-world queries, and generation guardrails to deliver factual, context-aware answers.

Knowledge-first design
  1. Organizes fragmented info into retrievable, structured formats.
  2. Prioritizes clarity over coverage - it only answers what it truly knows.
  3. Designed not for generality, but for precision in your domain.

Tailored for private domains where hallucination is unacceptable

Query-tuned embeddings
  1. Embeddings are generated from real-world, conversational phrasing.
  2. Handles synonyms, rewordings, and partial questions with accuracy.
  3. Learns and expands from actual user logs to fill coverage gaps over time

Optimized for how people actually ask.

Precision-first generation
  1. Produces natural language responses only when source material supports it.
  2. Responds with a polite “I'm not sure” when the knowledge isn't there.
  3. Maintains alignment with source intent - no speculation

Answers only when it's sure.
